Economic Sciences

Announcement of the 2024 Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel

Daron Acemoglu
U.S.
Simon Johnson
U.S.
James A. Robinson
U.S.

For studies of how institutions are formed and affect prosperity

Daron Acemoglu and Simon Johnson are currently Professors at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), Cambridge, U.S.

James A. Robinson serves as a Professor at University of Chicago, U.S.

Their work examines the reasons for the differences in prosperity between countries, one of them being institutions introduced through colonisation. It focuses on extractive and inclusive colonial institutions that have reversed the fortunes of colonies, making poor colonies richer and rich colonies poorer.

Peace

Announcement of the 2024 Nobel Peace Prize

Nihon Hidankyo
Japanese Organisation

For its efforts to achieve a world free of nuclear weapons and for demonstrating through witness testimony that nuclear weapons must never be used again.

Nihon Hidankyo is the only nation-wide organization of A-bomb survivors of Hiroshima and Nagasaki (Hibakusha). It has member organizations in all 47 Japanese prefectures, thus representing almost all organized Hibakusha. Its officials and members are all Hibakusha. The total number of the surviving Hibakusha living in Japan is 174,080, as of March 2016. There are several thousands of more Hibakusha living in Korea and other parts of the world outside Japan. HIDANKYO is cooperating with those organizations in their work for the defense of the living and rights of these people.

Literature

Announcement of the 2024 Nobel Prize in Literature

Han Kang
South Korean

For her intense poetic prose that confronts historical traumas and exposes the fragility of human life.

Han Kang was born in 1970 in the South Korean city of Gwangju before moving with her family to Seoul. She comes from a literary background, her father Han Seung-won being a reputed novelist. Alongside her writing, she has also devoted herself to art and music, which is reflected throughout her entire literary production.

Han Kang began her career in 1993 with the publication of a number of poems in the Literature and Society. Her prose debut came in 1995 with the short story collection. Han Kang’s major international breakthrough came with the novel The Vegetarian in 2015. In her oeuvre, Han Kang confronts historical traumas and invisible sets of rules and, in each of her works, exposes the fragility of human life.

Chemistry

Announcement of the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry

David Baker
U.S.
Demis Hassabis
U.K.
John M. Jumper
U.K.

Baker was awarded the prize “for computational protein design”

Hassabis and Jumper were awarded the prize “for protein structure prediction”

David Baker is a Professor at the University of Washington. He succeeded with the almost impossible feat of building entirely new kinds of proteins. Since 2003, Baker has been using amino acids to create new proteins. These have uses in pharmaceuticals, vaccines, nanomaterials and as sensors.

Demis Hassabis is the CEO of Google DeepMind, London, UK. John M. Jumper is a Senior Research Scientist at the same organisation. Hassabis and Jumper have developed an AI model to solve a 50-year-old problem: predicting proteins’ complex structures. These discoveries hold enormous potential.

Physics

Announcement of the 2024 Nobel Prize in Physics

John J. Hopfield
U.S.
Geoffrey E. Hinton
Canada

For the foundational discoveries and inventions that enable machine learning with artificial neural networks

John J. Hopfield, born in 1933 in Chicago, Illinois, United States, earned his PhD from Cornell University in 1958. He is currently a Professor at Princeton University in New Jersey, USA. He invented a network, the Hopfield network, that uses a method for saving and recreating patterns.

Geoffrey E. Hinton, born in 1947 in London, UK, received his PhD from the University of Edinburgh in 1978. He is currently a Professor at the University of Toronto, Canada. Mr. Hinton invented a method that can autonomously find properties in data, and so perform tasks such as identifying specific elements in pictures.

Physiology & Medicine

Announcement of the 2024 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine

Victor Ambros
U.S.
Gary Ruvkun
U.S.

The prize was awarded for the discovery of microRNA and its role in post-transcriptional gene regulation

Victor Ambros, born in 1953 in Hanover, New Hampshire, U.S., earned his PhD from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1979, where he also conducted postdoctoral research until 1985. In 1985, he became a Principal Investigator at Harvard University. From 1992 to 2007, he served as a professor at Dartmouth Medical School. Currently, he holds the position of Silverman Professor of Natural Science at the University of Massachusetts Medical School in Worcester, MA.

Gary Ruvkun, born in 1952 in Berkeley, California, U.S., earned his PhD from Harvard University in 1982. Following this, he completed a postdoctoral fellowship at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) from 1982 to 1985. In 1985, he became a Principal Investigator at Massachusetts General Hospital and Harvard Medical School, where he currently serves as a Professor of Genetics.
